History

The island was originally inhabited by the Taino people. In 1494, Christopher Columbus reached Jamaica, and the island later became a British colony. African culture became a major part of Jamaican identity due to the transatlantic slave trade.

Jamaica gained independence from Britain on August 6, 1962. Today, Jamaica is known for its strong cultural identity, global musical influence, and remarkable athletes.

Geography

Jamaica is the third-largest island in the Caribbean. It is known for its mountains, rivers, waterfalls, and tropical beaches.

The capital city is Kingston. Popular tourist destinations include Montego Bay, Negril, Port Antonio, and Ocho Rios.
